Mission & Objectives

PhysioHead advances evidence-based physiotherapy, behavioral, and digital non-pharmacological approaches in headache care through international collaboration, research, education, and public engagement.

Rationale and background

Headache disorders such as migraine and tension-type headache represent a significant global health burden. Pharmacological treatments remain central to management, but many patients experience suboptimal response, intolerance, contraindications, or prefer non-pharmacological options.

Physiotherapy and behavioral interventions, including manual therapy, exercise-based rehabilitation, postural correction, cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), mindfulness-based approaches, Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), schema therapy, and digitally delivered care, have growing evidence of benefit. These approaches are still underrepresented in structured education, research priorities, and clinical guidelines. PhysioHead fills that gap with a dedicated multidisciplinary platform.

Mission statement

Advance evidence-based physiotherapy, behavioral, and digital non-pharmacological approaches in headache care through international collaboration, research, education, and public engagement.

Objectives

1

Advance the evidence base

Promote scientific research on physiotherapy and behavioral interventions in headache disorders.

2

Strengthen collaboration

Connect neurologists, physiotherapists, psychologists, and allied health professionals.

3

Shape clinical guidance

Develop consensus-based guidance and position statements.

4

Integrate care models

Embed non-pharmacological strategies into comprehensive headache care.

5

Elevate education & awareness

Support clinicians, patients, and policymakers with updated knowledge.

6

Expand access

Scale remote and digitally delivered care options worldwide.

Scope of interest

  • Migraine
  • Tension-type headache
  • Cervicogenic headache
  • Chronic headache disorders
  • Musculoskeletal contributors to headache
  • Behavioral comorbidities (stress, anxiety, depression, sleep disturbance)
  • Digital, telehealth, and hybrid non-pharmacological care models

Alignment with strategic goals

PhysioHead aligns with international headache society priorities by promoting equitable and accessible care, supporting education and capacity building, encouraging global collaboration, and advancing innovation in headache research and management.

Sustainability & future directions

The SIG will operate with transparent leadership rotation, open membership, regular reporting, and ongoing recruitment of early-career professionals and allied health experts.
