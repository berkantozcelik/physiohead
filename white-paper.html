Beyond Pharmacology in Chronic Headache Care

Contributing Authors: Members of the PhysioHead Collaborative Group

Physiotherapy, behavioral, and rehabilitative approaches alongside pharmacology. This page presents the current draft scope of the PhysioHead White Paper.

Open PDF draft
Status: Draft Outline Format: White Paper Audience: Clinicians, researchers, policymakers, educators
Executive Summary
  • Unmet needs in chronic headache management.
  • Why pharmacology alone is not enough.
  • Growing role of physiotherapy and behavioral care.
  • Actionable clinical and systems-level conclusions.
Core Sections

Draft chapter structure.

1. Introduction: Why This White Paper Now?

  • Global burden of chronic headache disorders.
  • Guideline and real-world care gaps.
  • Rising patient demand for non-pharmacological options.
  • Purpose and scope of this White Paper.

2. Chronic Headache Disorders: Clinical Context

  • Chronic migraine and chronic tension-type headache.
  • Cervicogenic and overlapping headache syndromes.
  • Comorbidities affecting rehabilitation outcomes.

3. Rationale for Non-Pharmacological and Rehabilitative Care

  • Limitations of long-term pharmacological strategies.
  • Patient preference, adherence, and safety.
  • Complementarity with acute and preventive medication.
  • Special-interest populations: children, elderly, pregnancy, breastfeeding.

4. Physiotherapy Approaches in Chronic Headache

  • Posture, movement, and cervical biomechanics.
  • Manual therapy and soft tissue interventions.
  • Exercise-based rehabilitation.
  • Evidence summary and clinical pearls.

5. Behavioral and Psychological Interventions

  • CBT, ACT, schema therapy, EMDR, mindfulness-based interventions.
  • Stress, anxiety, depression, and pain coping frameworks.

6. Mechanistic Perspectives

  • Central sensitization and neuroplasticity.
  • Trigeminocervical and musculoskeletal pathways.
  • Brain-body interaction models.

7. Digital, Tele-Rehabilitation, and Hybrid Models

  • Digital physiotherapy and remote behavioral interventions.
  • Hybrid pathways with equity, access, and scalability focus.

8. Integrating Non-Pharmacological Care into Pathways

  • Multidisciplinary clinics and personalized stepwise models.
  • Allied health roles and coordination with pharmacological care.

9. Patient-Centered Outcomes and Real-World Evidence

  • Function, quality of life, adherence, and satisfaction.
  • Cultural/regional considerations and optional health economics.

10. Research Gaps and Future Directions

  • Methodological limitations and outcome standardization.
  • Mechanistic priorities and implementation science needs.

11. Consensus Statements and Key Recommendations

  • What clinicians should implement now.
  • What researchers should prioritize.
  • What health systems should support.

12. Call to Action + Appendices

  • Calls to clinicians, researchers, societies, journals, and guideline bodies.
  • Appendix A-D: glossary, intervention tables, minimum outcomes, contributors.
  • Contributor list link: Members.
